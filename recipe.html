Wow this was simply fantastic. I had bought am Aberdeen Angus tomahawk steak that came in at around 900g and had been dry aged for 32 days. Cooking

Ingredients

  • 1 large un-smoked gammon
  • ½ a cup of sliced ginger
  • small bunch of sage
  • 2 carrots, roughly chopped
  • 4 stalks of celery, chopped
  • Nutmeg
  • 1 stick of cinnamon
  • 2tsb of cloves
  • 1 onion quartered
  • 2 litres of ginger beer
  • 250ml of chicken stock

Ingredients

For the glaze

  • 1tsb of salt
  • 4tsb of Mustard Powder
  • 250g of apricot jam

Method

  • Add all the ingredients to a large stock pot
  • Cover with chicken stock and ginger beer
  • Bring to a boil then reduce to a simmer
  • Simmer for 30 to 45 minutes
  • Remove from the heat and wrap the pot in a blanket making sure the lid is tightly sealed
  • Leave overnight
  • Remove the gammon from the pot
  • Carefully slice the skin off leaving as much fat on the gammon as possible
  • Prepare the glaze
  • Combine the ingredients for the glaze in a saucepan over a low heat stirring continuously
  • Once combined remove from the heat
  • Preheat the oven to 220°C
  • Cut a diamond pattern into the fat of the gammon
  • Stud the gammon with cloves
  • Baste the gammon with the glaze
  • Roast the gammon for 30 to minutes removing the gammon every 10 minutes to apply more glaze
  • Allow the gammon to rest before serving
